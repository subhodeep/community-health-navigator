# Air Quality (AQI) Health Advisory — Franklin Ridge County

The county publishes a daily Air Quality Index (AQI) per district. This guide
explains what the numbers mean and what to do.

## AQI bands
| AQI | Level | Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| 0–50 | Good | No precautions needed. |
| 51–100 | Moderate | Unusually sensitive people should consider shorter outdoor exertion. |
| 101–150 | Unhealthy for sensitive groups | People with asthma, COPD, heart disease, children, and 65+ should limit prolonged outdoor exertion. Keep rescue inhalers at hand. |
| 151–200 | Unhealthy | Everyone should reduce prolonged outdoor exertion. Sensitive groups should stay indoors with windows closed. |
| 201+ | Very unhealthy | Avoid outdoor activity. Use HEPA filtration if available. Check on elderly neighbors. |

## For residents with asthma or COPD
- On days above AQI 100, take medications as prescribed and keep windows closed
  during peak afternoon hours.
- Free HEPA filter loans are available at district wellness centers for Wellness
  Program members during declared smoke events.
- If you use a rescue inhaler more than every 4 hours, or have chest tightness
  that does not improve, seek medical care immediately.

## Alerts
Residents can subscribe to district AQI alerts through the Community Health
Navigator assistant or the County Health Line (555-0142). A common threshold for
sensitive groups is **AQI 100**; for the general population **AQI 150**.

During declared smoke events, cooling/clean-air shelters open at all district
wellness centers from 10am to 8pm.
